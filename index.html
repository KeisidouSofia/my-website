Plastic

  • Rinse containers to remove food residue and prevent contamination.
  • Remove caps and labels if your local recycling program requires it.
  • Flatten bottles to save space in recycling bins.
  • Avoid single-use plastics like straws, plastic bags, and cutlery; consider reusable alternatives.
  • Check recycling codes (#1 PET, #2 HDPE, etc.) to ensure your local facility accepts them.

Paper

  • Recycle clean office paper, magazines, envelopes, and junk mail (remove plastic windows from envelopes).
  • Flatten cardboard boxes and break them down to save space.
  • Shredded paper can usually be recycled; check local rules.
  • Avoid tissues, paper towels, or napkins—they are often contaminated with food or liquids.

Metal

  • Recycle aluminum cans, tin cans, and foil.
  • Rinse thoroughly to remove residue and prevent odors.
  • Remove any non-metal parts like plastic lids or paper labels if required.
  • Consider repurposing scrap metal for DIY projects if recycling isn’t immediately convenient.
  • Check if your local facility accepts aerosol cans—they often require special handling.

Electronics

  • Old phones, laptops, batteries, and appliances should be taken to certified e-waste collection centers.
  • Remove personal data from devices before recycling.
  • Some electronics retailers offer trade-in or recycling programs.
  • Never dispose of electronics in regular trash—they contain hazardous materials like lead and mercury.
  • Consider donating working electronics to charities or schools to extend their life.

Glass

  • Recycle bottles and jars by rinsing and removing lids.
  • Avoid broken glass in curbside bins if your program does not accept it; take it to a dedicated drop-off location.
  • Colored glass may need to be separated (check local rules).
  • Do not mix ceramics, mirrors, or light bulbs with glass recycling—they require special disposal.
  • Reduce glass waste by reusing jars for storage or DIY projects.
